Ensuring Transparency, Ethics, and Trust at Bhramara Educational Society

Consultancy Policy

Bhramara Educational Society encourages its faculty members to participate in consultancy projects with industrial and government organizations. We believe that such interactions enhance the professional standard of our faculty and provide practical exposure to our students. The primary objective is to utilize the institutional expertise for the benefit of the industry while maintaining the academic integrity of the trust.

All consultancy assignments must be officially registered with the Society’s Research and Development (R&D) Cell. Faculty members are permitted to spend a limited amount of working hours on these projects, provided it does not affect their teaching schedules. The use of institutional laboratories, software, and equipment for consultancy is encouraged, subject to nominal usage charges determined by the board.

The revenue sharing model follows a 60:40 ratio, where 60% of the net profit goes to the lead consultant (faculty) and 40% is retained by the Society for infrastructure maintenance. In cases where institutional resources are not utilized, the society may increase the faculty's share. All financial transactions must be transparent and conducted through the official society accounts.

Intellectual Property (IP) generated through consultancy remains a joint asset between the Society and the external client, depending on the initial agreement. Faculty members must ensure that they do not enter into any agreement that restricts the academic use of research findings. Confidentiality of client data is mandatory and must be protected through formal non-disclosure agreements.

The society reserves the right to terminate any consultancy project if it is found to be unethical or against the public interest. Every consultant is required to submit a quarterly progress report and a final project completion document. This policy ensures that the institution remains at the forefront of technical and social innovation while rewarding merit.

Refund Policy

Bhramara Educational Society follows a transparent and fair refund policy for all its institutions. We understand that circumstances may change, and students may need to withdraw their admission. However, as an educational trust, we plan our infrastructure and faculty recruitment based on confirmed enrollments, which necessitates a structured refund timeline.

If a student requests a withdrawal before the official commencement of the academic session, a full refund of the tuition fee will be processed after deducting a nominal administrative processing fee (typically ₹5000). The registration fee and prospectus fee are non-refundable under any circumstances as they cover the initial documentation costs.

For withdrawals made within 15 days of the session starting, 80% of the tuition fee will be refunded. If the request is made between 15 and 30 days, 50% will be refunded. No refund requests will be entertained after 30 days of the academic session’s commencement, as the seat would remain vacant for the entire year, causing a financial loss to the trust.

Refunds in special cases, such as medical emergencies or government-directed cancellations, will be evaluated on a case-by-case basis by the Executive Committee. Students must submit a formal application along with the original fee receipts and valid supporting documents to the Registrar’s office to initiate the process.

Approved refunds will be processed via bank transfer only to the account of the parent or guardian registered during admission. The processing time is usually 15 to 20 business days. Any bank charges or transaction fees incurred during the refund process will be borne by the applicant. We aim to ensure that every refund is handled with dignity and professional efficiency.
